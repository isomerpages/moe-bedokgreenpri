<p><strong>Vision</strong> 
<br>To grow confident and creative learners who express ideas and enjoy learning
through Art and Music.</p>
<p><strong>Mission</strong>
</p>
<p>We help every student to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>explore and express ideas through art and music.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>build skills and confidence by creating, performing and sharing, and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>appreciate different cultures, music and art.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Through hands-on learning, authentic experiences and purposeful use of
technology, we support every child in discovering their creative voice.</p>
<h3><strong>Art Education</strong></h3>
<p>Art education provides students with opportunities to <strong>observe, imagine, experiment and express</strong> their
ideas visually. Our Art curriculum is aligned to the Primary Art Syllabus
and is designed to be engaging, relevant and inclusive.</p>

<h3><strong>Music Education</strong></h3>
<p>Music education develops students as <strong>active listeners, performers and creators</strong>.
Our Music programme emphasises joyful participation, strong musical foundations
and opportunities for creative expression.</p>
<h4><strong>Key Features of Our Music Programme</strong></h4>
<table style="minWidth: 25px">
<colgroup>
<col>
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p><strong>1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Active and Hands-On Music-Making</strong>
</p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>Lessons focus on singing, playing instruments, listening and creating
music. Students experience music through both vocals and instruments, building
confidence and enjoyment.</p>
<p><strong>Progressive Instrumental Learning</strong>
</p>
<p>Students learn instruments in a structured and age-appropriate manner:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Primary 1–2: Resonator bells and unpitched percussion</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Primary 3–4: Keyboard and ukulele</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Primary 5: Ukulele and digital music tools</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Primary 6: Digital music creation using GarageBand</p>
</li>
</ul>
<table style="minWidth: 25px">
<colgroup>
<col>
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p><strong>2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Strong Musical Foundations (Kodály-informed Approach)</strong>
</p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>Our teachers apply strategies from the Kodály approach, using singing,
movement and listening to help students develop pitch, rhythm and musical
understanding in a supportive and engaging way.</p>
<table style="minWidth: 25px">
<colgroup>
<col>
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p><strong>3.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Use of Technology to Enhance Learning</strong>
</p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>Technology is used meaningfully to support music learning. Performances
are recorded to provide feedback, and older students explore music composition
and arrangement using digital tools such as GarageBand.</p>
<table style="minWidth: 25px">
<colgroup>
<col>
</colgroup>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td rowspan="1" colspan="1">
<p><strong>4.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Performance and Sharing Opportunities</strong>
</p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>Students are given opportunities to perform and share their learning during
class activities and school events, helping them develop confidence, teamwork
and communication skills.</p>
